Quantum Entanglement

link

Original Source: Scientific American - Physics Journal

lightbulb

Simple Explanation

Imagine you have two magic coins. When you flip one and it lands on heads, the other one automatically lands on heads too, even if it's on the other side of the universe. They are connected in a way that scientists call "entangled." No matter how far apart they are, what happens to one instantly affects the other.

format_list_bulleted

3 Key Points

1

Particles can become linked so that their states are dependent on each other, no matter the distance.

2

Measuring one particle instantly determines the state of the other particle it is entangled with.

3

This happens faster than the speed of light, which Albert Einstein famously called "spooky action at a distance."

1 Example

"Think of a pair of matching socks. If you put one on your left foot in New York, the other sock (wherever it is in the world) instantly becomes the 'right foot' sock. In the quantum world, it's like the socks didn't even decide which foot they were for until you actually put one on."
